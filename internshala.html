Internshala App

App Design

Improving the internship finding & applying experience for 6 million+ college students

Role

Product Design and Design systems

Timeline

Dec 2019 - Feb 2020

Company

Internshala

Internshala is the largest internships & training platform in India with 6 million+ active students & 100 thousand+ companies / startups. With great numbers come great bandwidths.

During my internship, I was tasked with the product design for Internshala Android app, based on the new design system. It was focused on clear information, intuitive interactions and a unified experience.

The team included two Android developers, a product manager and a design lead.

New Design

Problem

Information: Redundant and repetitive data increased cognitive load while browsing. Harder to navigated through information quickly due to lack of focus.

Complex: Lack of clarity & heirarchy in forms and cards were obstacle to user flow. Intricate iconography at smaller sizes was illegible and hard to understand.

Consistency: There were major inconsistencies in input fields, buttons and action flows. UI Audit & study opened up a spectrum of opportunities.

Internal UX: The Internshala app wasn’t designed fully in the earlier versions. There was a clear design debt, as it was fragemented and undocumented.

Design Process

After establishing the design goals, constraints (a multitude of those) and guardrails, I followed double diamond model.

Being an agile company, we didn’t have a large team and much time on our hands. I worked through an iterative process of ideation through implementation.

Unified system for cards

The cards in Internshala app are a system-wide component that display key informations of an internship. There was no unified pattern and instances varied over screens.

The end-goal is an outcome that is rich and sophisticated enough to be able to behave like a system, instead of being like an object.

A structured approach was created to form a functioning system of cards that put information first. Over various iterations, I experimented with data heirarchy, layout and deducting redundant labels to reach a final pattern.

Following the pattern created in the main card, all cases of the card were designed. Each card is a comprehensive component in Figma with nested nuance and auto-layout properties to scale effectively.

Informing via bite sized chips

The informative blocks for the details section of cards are called chips. They are fast-paced & minimal data bites that I used to inform users intuitively.

Details chip informs regarding the internship and it’s various details such as location, stipend and applicants. Meanwhile, the status chip is for tracking the user’s application status. It is served in tints accordingly.
