Internshala App

App Design

Improving the internship finding & applying experience for 6 million+ college students

Role

Product Design and Design systems

Timeline

Dec 2019 - Feb 2020

Company

Internshala

Internshala is the largest internships & training platform in India with 6 million+ active students & 100 thousand+ companies / startups. With great numbers come great bandwidths.

During my internship, I was tasked with the product design for Internshala Android app, based on the new design system. The redesign was focused on clear information, intuitive interactions and a unified experience throughout the app. The team included two developers, a product manager and a design lead.

Right from the start I was actively contributing to the new Internshala design system, building the style guide library comprehensively on Figma. This was the first time a consistent pattern was designed by the team and hence it required multiple UI audits & testing. After 2 months of effective work, we had a progressive system with defined components. At this point, it was decided to start with the product design for Internshala Android app, reflecting the fresh new visual style.

Problem

Information: Redundant and repetitive data increased cognitive load while browsing. Harder to read through byte-sized information quickly.

Complex: Lack of clarity & heirarchy in forms and cards were obstacle to user flow. Intricate iconography at smaller sizes was illegible and hard to understand.

Consistency: The Internshala app wasn’t designed fully in the earlier versions. Few screens were directly coded by developers and weren’t available as prototypes.

Design Process

After establishing the design goals, constraints and guardrails, I followed a double diamond process.

Unified system for cards

The cards in Internshala app are a system-wide component that display key informations of an internship. There was no unified pattern and instances varied over screens.

The end-goal is an outcome that is rich and sophisticated enough to be able to behave like a system, instead of being like an object.

A structured approach was created to form a functioning system of cards that put information first. Over various iterations, I experimented with data heirarchy, layout and deducting redundant labels.

Following the pattern created in the main card, all cases of the card were designed. Each card is a comprehensive component in Figma with nested nuance and auto-layout properties to scale effectively.
